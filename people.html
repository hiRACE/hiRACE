Kate Fell

PhD Researcher

Kate Fell
PhD Researcher

  • James Watt School of Engineering
  • University of Glasgow
  • K.Fell.1@research.gla.ac.uk

Background
  • MSc Pharmaceutical Analysis – University of Strathclyde, Glasgow
  • Internship at the European Directorate for the Quality of Medicines and Healthcare (EDQM)
  • BSc (Hons) Forensic Investigation – Glasgow Caledonian University
  • Associate Member of the Royal Society of Chemistry (AMRSC)
Research Vision
I am a PhD student working within the HiRACE Laboratory at Glasgow University. I have always been interested in environmental pollution; and ways to combat it; and therefore, combined my interest with my skillset and targeted my PhD on pharmaceutical micropollutants. My current work focusses on optimising derivatisation of pharmaceutical compounds (PhCs) for analysis by gas chromatography-mass spectrometry (GC-MS).
The key aim of my research is to develop a method which can be used for non-targeted analysis and identification of PhCs in environmental matrices, such as wastewater. This is important as PhCs are prevalent within the environment, and can lead to damaging consequences, including an increase in anti-microbial resistance. If a method of identifying all possible PhCs within a water source is obtained; this is the first step to removal and regulation of these micropollutants.
My vision is that my research can be used as a reference point to further develop analytical detection methods for micropollutants, which will lead to the regulation of pharmaceutical compounds which are being released into the environment. My goal is to create a cleaner, safer environment through the outcomes of my research.

Key Skills
  • Gas Chromatography
  • Mass Spectrometry
  • Principle Component Analysis
  • Pharmaceutical Analysis

Celeste Felion

PhD Researcher

Celeste Felion
PhD Researcher

  • James Watt School of Engineering
  • University of Glasgow
  • C.Felion.1@research.gla.ac.uk

Research Vision
I am a PhD student researching new analytical methods to address key challenges in the remediation of environmental emerging contaminants. My current research is focused on measuring natural estrogens and PPCPs by three spectroscopic techniques: fluorimetry, absorbance spectroscopy, and Raman spectroscopy. These techniques are faster and less expensive than established trace analysis platforms, such as GCMS and LCMS. By demonstrating the utility of these methods in monitoring bacterial degradation of micropollutants, I hope to determine if the designed methods are fit-for-purpose in both efficiently isolating micropollutant-degrading bacteria and efficiently measuring micropollutant concentrations in bacteria cultures.

Key Skills

  • Molecular Biology
  • Microbial Metabolism
